Smart Toilet

June 2019 - Present

This project is a work in progress and I can't share everything on it yet due to patent development and other concerns. This page will be updated when possible.

Overview

I am leading the mechanical development for a smart toilet biomedical device. I've been using and developing my skills in rapid prototyping, design, machining, and sensor technology. I have designed and constructed specialized mechanisms, automated testing systems, and device prototypes.

This project is being developed as a collaboration between Duke University's DesignHub program and the David Lab at Duke University.

Hinge

I helped design a hinge for the device that reduces or eliminates the load put on it in specific orientations. I also am refining the design in order to be compatible with advanced fabrication methods.

Testing Rig

I designed and constructed a testing rig that has allowed for ease of research and development for our device. This rig includes mechanisms to simulate specific human bodily functions, and automates data collection for testing of our device.

Sensor Fittings

I have designed and fabricated a variety of sensor fittings and clips that allow the sensors to interface with our device properly.
