Automatic Skunk Feeder

We created a set of portable, modular, and easily-sanitized devices to dispense food for small terrestrial animals at random time intervals in various locations within an enclosure.

The Problem

The Museum of Life and Science in Durham, NC houses a skunk named Florian. In order to provide food for the skunk, museum keepers take him out and scatter food around the enclosure. This method is inefficient and has led to Florianâ€™s unhealthy association of humans with food. The museum staff wanted a feeding method that mimics the natural unpredictability of food in the wild.

Team Members

The design team for this project was assembled through an introductory engineering design class.

  • Mohsin Haider (myself)
  • Emily Ma
  • Bailey Patrick
  • Jason Dong

The team was guided by our TA Kayla Wright-Freeman, our instructor Dr. Rebecca Simmons, and our Technical Mentor Dr. Neil Simmons.

The Solution

We created a set of four independently-operated capsules that release food from a hinged bottom flap.

Device Structure

  • Four laser-cut acrylic walls fused together with Weld-On
  • A laser-cut acrylic lid
  • Two 3D-printed holders that house the electronics and provide a channel for the lid to cover the top of the device
  • Two neodynium magnets screwed into the lid
  • A laser-cut acrylic hinged bottom flap
