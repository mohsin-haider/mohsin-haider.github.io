Automatic Skunk Feeder

August 2018 - December 2018

We created a set of portable, modular, and easily-sanitized devices to dispense food for small terrestrial animals at random time intervals in various locations within an enclosure.

The Problem

The Museum of Life and Science in Durham, NC houses a skunk named Florian. In order to provide food for the skunk, museum keepers take him out and scatter food around the enclosure. This method is inefficient and has led to Florianâ€™s unhealthy association of humans with food. The museum staff wanted a feeding method that mimics the natural unpredictability of food in the wild.

The Solution

We created a set of four independently-operated capsules that release food from a hinged bottom flap. This bottom flap is held together by an electromagnet which is deactivated at the time of food dispersal. The devices can be attached magnetically to the ceiling of the enclosure in various spots in order to change the location of food dispersal. An Arduino controls the timing of the food drop, providing a more natural feeding experience for Florian.

Device Structure

  • Four laser-cut acrylic walls fused together with Weld-On
  • A laser-cut acrylic lid
  • Two 3D-printed holders that house the electronics and provide a channel for the lid to cover the top of the device
  • Two neodynium magnets screwed into the lid
  • A laser-cut acrylic hinged bottom flap

Electronics

Component Role
DS3231 Real-Time Clock Module
  • Keeps track of real-world time
  • Runs off of a rechargeable 3V battery
Arduino Uno R3
  • Controls power output to electromagnet
  • Reads time from RTC module
  • Powered by mobile power bank
Grove Electromagnet
  • Magnetized to steel strip on bottom lip of device hinge
  • Snaps into the Arduino using the Grove Shield
  • Powered by the Arduino 5V pin
