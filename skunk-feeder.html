Automatic Skunk Feeder

We created a set of portable, modular, and easily-sanitized devices to dispense food for small terrestrial animals at random time intervals in various locations within an enclosure

The Problem

The Museum of Life and Science in Durham, NC houses a skunk named Florian. In order to provide food for the skunk, museum keepers take him out and scatter food around the enclosure. This method is inefficient and has led to Florian’s unhealthy association of humans with food. The museum staff wants a feeding method that mimics the natural unpredictability of food in the wild. The solution must be easy to clean and easily maintained. The museum cleans most of its equipment in a sanitizer which reaches temperatures around 200°F.

Team Members

The design team for this project was assembled through an introductory engineering design class.

  • Mohsin Haider (myself)

The Solution

We created a set of four independently-operated capsules that release food from a hinged bottom flap.
