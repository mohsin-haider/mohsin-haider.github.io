Electric Penny Board

September 2019 - Present

We're creating a bluetooth-enabled, unintrusive motor attachment that allows users to motorize their Penny boards.

The Problem

My friend Fran and I both like to ride penny boards, but whenever we want to go to his apartment, we can't ride them up the hill leading to it, so we just end up carrying the boards.

The Goal

Modify our penny boards so that we can make it up the hill leading to Fran's apartment.

The Team

  • Mohsin Haider (myself): A novice Penny-boarder.
  • Fran Romano: My friend and also an expert Penny-boarder (check him out here).
  • Together we're two Mechanical Engineering students looking for a fun and exciting way to apply our technical skills.

Background

  • Penny boards are small skateboards with a 22-inch flexible plastic deck.
  • They are usually used for cruising on flatter surfaces.
  • Because they are smaller, they are often valued for their light weight and portability.

The Solution

We created a motor attachment for our penny boards that can attached and removed without permanent or irreversible modifications to the boards. The attachment connects a motor to one of the rear wheels using a sprocket that is inserted into the wheel along with a belt and a key that is attached to the motor shaft.

Key Features

There are some features that set our project apart from other similar products that we have seen on the market.

  • Controlled with a smartphone's volume buttons using Bluetooth.
  • Can be attached and removed without irreversible modifications to the board.
  • Builds off of existing penny board parts.

How It Works

Electronics

Component: Smartphone HC-05 Bluetooth Module Arduino Nano ESC DC Brushless Motor
Role: User uses volume buttons to input their desired speed Recieves wireless serial data from phone and feeds it into the Arduino Converts the serial data to microseconds, which the ESC can understand Turns the microseconds into a throttle curve and activates the motor Rotates its shaft, which rotates the wheel and moves the board

Latest Progress

Our most recent progress was a successful run of the motor attachment on the skateboard using the battery as a power source and an Android phone as an input device.
